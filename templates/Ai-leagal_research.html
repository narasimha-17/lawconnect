Explain the concept of 'Doctrine of Lis Pendens' in Indian Property Law.
The Doctrine of Lis Pendens is captured under Section 52 of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882. It prevents the transfer of property while it is the subject of ongoing litigation.
Key Case Law: Jayaram Mudaliar v. Ayyaswami (1972) - The Supreme Court clarified that the purpose of Section 52 is not to annul the conveyance, but only to render it subservient to the rights of the parties to the litigation.
